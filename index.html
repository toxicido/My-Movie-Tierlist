My Movie Tierlist

Welcome to My Movie Tier List!

Movies have a unique way of captivating us, making us laugh, cry, and even question the world around us. As a passionate movie enthusiast, I’ve decided to rank my all-time favorite films based on personal enjoyment, storytelling, cinematography, and overall impact. This tier list is not just about box office numbers or critic scores—it's about the films that left a lasting impression on me.

Whether you agree or disagree with my rankings, I hope this list sparks some fun discussions and maybe even helps you discover a new favorite movie! So, grab some popcorn, sit back, and explore my movie tier list.

Let's dive in!

5. The Dark Knight (2008)

Christopher Nolan’s The Dark Knight is an absolute masterpiece that redefined the superhero genre

With its gripping narrative, breathtaking cinematography, and unforgettable performances, this film is more than just a Batman movie—it’s a crime thriller that keeps you on the edge of your seat from start to finish.

Heath Ledger’s portrayal of the Joker is legendary, bringing a chilling and chaotic energy that elevates the entire film

Every moment is packed with tension, action, and emotional depth, making it one of the greatest films of all time

Ranking: 8,5/10

4. Her (2013)

Spike Jonze’s Her is a beautifully crafted, thought-provoking film that explores love, loneliness, and human connection in the digital age.

Joaquin Phoenix delivers an incredibly nuanced performance as Theodore, a man who forms a deep emotional bond with an artificial intelligence, voiced brilliantly by Scarlett Johansson.

The film’s stunning cinematography, warm color palette, and evocative score create a dreamlike atmosphere that perfectly complements its deeply emotional narrative.

Her is not just a film about technology—it’s a poignant meditation on what it means to love and be loved, making it one of the most touching and original movies of the decade.

Ranking: 8,5/10

3. The Wolf of The Wall Street (2013)

Martin Scorsese’s The Wolf of Wall Street is a wild, electrifying ride through the excess and chaos of Wall Street in the 1990s

Leonardo DiCaprio delivers a career-defining performance as Jordan Belfort, a stockbroker who rises to immense wealth through fraud and corruption.

The film’s razor-sharp script, fast-paced editing, and dark humor make it an exhilarating watch from start to finish.

With unforgettable scenes, outrageous antics, and a deep exploration of greed and moral decay, The Wolf of Wall Street is an unfiltered look at the highs and lows of unchecked ambition.

It’s a film that entertains, shocks, and leaves a lasting impression long after the credits roll.

Ranking: 9/10

2. The Naked Gun (1988)

David Zucker’s The Naked Gun: From the Files of Police Squad! is a comedic gem that delivers non-stop laughs and absurdly brilliant gags.

Starring Leslie Nielsen as the clueless yet determined detective Frank Drebin, the film masterfully blends slapstick humor, clever wordplay, and over-the-top action.

Every scene is packed with hilarious visual jokes and memorable one-liners that make it one of the most beloved comedies of all time.

Whether it’s the ridiculous crime investigations or the exaggerated action sequences, The Naked Gun remains a timeless classic that proves comedy can be both smart and ridiculously fun.

Ranking: 9,5/10

1. The Green Mile (1999)

Frank Darabont’s The Green Mile is an emotionally powerful and deeply moving film that explores themes of justice, compassion, and the supernatural.

Based on Stephen King’s novel, the story follows Paul Edgecomb (Tom Hanks), a death row prison guard, and John Coffey (Michael Clarke Duncan), a gentle giant with a miraculous gift.

The film’s slow-burn storytelling, heart-wrenching performances, and thought-provoking moral dilemmas make it an unforgettable experience.

The Green Mile is more than just a prison drama—it’s a profound meditation on humanity, kindness, and the extraordinary hidden within the ordinary.

Ranking: 10/10

Conclusion
